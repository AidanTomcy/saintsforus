---
title: St. Agnes of Rome
lifetime: 291-304
---

St. Agnes was a virgin martyr, venerated as a saint in the Roman Catholic Church,
Eastern Orthodox Church, the Anglican Communion and Lutheranism. St. Agnes is one of several virgin
martyrs commemorated by name in the Canon of the Mass. She is depicted in art with a lamb,
evoking her name, which resembles the latin word for lamb, "Agnus." She is also shown with a
martyr's palm. St. Agnes is the patron saint of girls and chastity.
